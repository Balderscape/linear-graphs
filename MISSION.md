# Mission: Linear Graphs for Amelia (Year 10)

## Why
Amelia (Year 10, strong at maths generally) is struggling with the linear graphs unit at school. The goal is for her to walk into class and assessments confident with straight-line graphs — turning her current wobble into a strength, with lots of easy wins along the way to rebuild confidence.

## Success looks like
- Reads `y = mx + c` and instantly knows what the line looks like (steepness, direction, where it crosses the y-axis)
- Plots any line given its equation, quickly and accurately
- Writes down the equation of a line shown on a graph
- Writes down the equation of a line from a table of x and y values
- Rearranges equations like `2y − 4x = 6` into `y = mx + c` form without hesitation
- Finds where two lines intersect (graphically and by algebra)
- Handles the edge cases calmly: `y = k`, `x = k`, and the axes themselves (`y = 0`, `x = 0`)

## Constraints
- Learner is 15; material must be fun, colourful and inviting — encouraging, never babyish
- Short graded lessons with quick wins; confidence-building is as important as content
- Lots of drill/practice with instant feedback (she learns by doing)
- Lessons run offline as local HTML files; parent (Paul) sets up the sessions
- UK/AU conventions: "maths", "gradient" (with "slope" noted as a synonym), y = mx + c (not y = mx + b)

## Out of scope
- Quadratics and other non-linear graphs
- Formal linear simultaneous-equation methods beyond what intersections need
- Coordinate geometry extensions (midpoints, distance, perpendicular gradients) — unless the school unit turns out to include them

Plotting Lines Like a Pro 📍

Given any equation, you’ll drop the dots and draw the line in under twenty seconds. Two steps. That’s the whole trick.

🎯 In this lesson

By the end you’ll plot any y = mx + c fast, with the two-step recipe: ① dot at c on the y-axis, then ② from there, across 1, up m for more dots. Join them with a ruler-straight line. Done.

The two-step recipe 🧑‍🍳

Let’s plot y = 2x − 1 together:

  1. Start at c. Here c = −1, so put a dot at (0, −1) on the y-axis. That’s the teal dot below.
  2. Step by m. Here m = 2, so from your dot go across 1, up 2 → dot at (1, 1). Do it again → dot at (2, 3).
  3. Join the dots with one dead-straight line, and extend it both ways. 📏

If m is a fraction like ½, use the Lesson 2 trick: step across 2, up 1 instead — same gradient, friendlier dots. And if m is negative, your steps go down, not up.

Backup plan: a table of values 🗂️

If the recipe ever deserts you (exam nerves are real), just feed the rule some x-values and compute y each time. For y = 2x − 1 with x = 0, 1, 2:

Plot (0, −1), (1, 1), (2, 3) — exactly the same three dots the recipe gave you, because it’s exactly the same maths. The recipe is just the table on fast-forward. ⏩

🎯 Click-to-plot

Your turn — no sliders, no safety net. I give you an equation; you click 3 different points that sit exactly on its line. Get all three and the line draws itself. Six lines to conquer, each a bit spicier than the last. 🌶️

Pro strategy: always claim the freebie first — the dot at c on the y-axis. Then step across 1, up m (across 2, up 1 for the half). For the last one, watch the grey grid corners: the line only ever touches corners it exactly passes through.
💚 Easy win: finish even one line here and you’ve done real plotting — the same skill as pencil-and-paper, just with instant marking. Finish all six and you can plot anything Year 10 throws at you.

Stretch & Flip: y = ax² 🤸

You’ve got the basic U. Now put a number in front of the x² and watch it squash, stretch, and flip upside-down.

🎯 In this lesson

One new number: a. You’ll learn how it makes the parabola skinny, fat, or flipped — and nothing else moves. (Next lesson we’ll add the up/down number.)

What a does

Start from y = x² and multiply the result by a: that’s y = ax². A quick way to feel it — at x = 1, y = a. So a is literally how high the curve is one step across:

  • a bigger than 1 (like 2 or 3) → tall & skinny (steeper).
  • a between 0 and 1 (like ½) → short & fat (wider).
  • a negative → the whole U flips into a ∩ (a frown 🙁). It now opens downward.

The vertex stays put at (0, 0) — a only bends the arms, it doesn’t move the curve.

🕹️ The stretch machine

The faint dashed curve is plain y = x², for comparison. Drag a and watch the solid one bend away from it.

Missions:
  1. Make the skinniest U you can, then the fattest.
  2. Flip it into a frown. What sign is a now?
  3. Set it so the curve sits right on top of the dashed one. What’s a?
  4. Make a frown that’s twice as steep as the dashed U.
💚 Easy win: size of a = skinny (big) or fat (small); sign of a = smile (+) or frown (−).

Meet the Line: y = mx + c 🎢

Two little numbers control every straight line in the universe. Today you take the controls.

🎯 In this lesson

By the end you’ll look at an equation like y = 2x + 3 and instantly know what its two numbers do — no plotting required. That’s it. One idea, properly owned.

A line is just a rule

An equation like y = 2x + 3 is a rule connecting x and y: “pick any x you like, double it, add 3 — that’s your y.” Every point that follows the rule gets drawn, and together they make a perfectly straight line.

y = mx + c

The two numbers have jobs:

  • m — the gradient (Americans say “slope”): how steep the line is, and which way it tilts.
  • c — the y-intercept: where the line crosses the y-axis.

Don’t memorise that — feel it. Playground time. 👇

🕹️ The line machine

Drag the sliders. Watch what each one does to the line.

Try these missions:
  1. Make the line completely flat. (Which slider did it? What value?)
  2. Make it slope downhill from left to right.
  3. Make it cross the y-axis at −4 — without touching the pink slider.
  4. Make the steepest possible uphill line.
  5. Slide c up and down: the line doesn’t tilt, it just elevator-rides. 🛗
💚 Easy win: if you did those five missions, you already understand y = mx + c better than half the class. The rest of this course is just practice.

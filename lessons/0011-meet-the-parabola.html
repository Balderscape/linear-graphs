Meet the Parabola 🥣

The shape of a thrown ball, a fountain, a satellite dish. We’ll build it the honest way — from a table of values, one point at a time.

🎯 In this lesson

You’ll meet y = x²: make a table, plot the points, and join them into a smooth U. By the end you’ll know its shape, its mirror symmetry, and its lowest point — the vertex.

The rule: square your x

Just like a line, a parabola is a rule connecting x and y. This time the rule is “square the x.” To draw any graph you can’t see yet, you do the same reliable thing you did with lines: build a table of values. Pick some x’s, work out each y:

x−3−2−10123
y = x²9410149

The trick everyone trips on: squaring a negative gives a positive. (−3)² = (−3) × (−3) = 9, not −9. So the y-row is never negative.

From table to curve

Now plot each pair as a point, then join them with a smooth curve — not straight lines between the dots (that’s the #1 mistake). Here are the table points, joined up:

Three things to notice on the picture:

  • It’s a symmetrical U called a parabola. The mirror line is the y-axis — x = −3 and x = 3 both land at 9.
  • The bottom of the U — the point (0, 0) here — is the vertex (the turning point).
  • The arms shoot upward forever, getting steeper and steeper.
💚 Easy win: to draw a parabola, make a table (remember negatives square to positives!), plot the points, and join them in a smooth U.

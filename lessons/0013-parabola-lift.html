Up & Down: y = ax² + k 🛗

Last parabola move. You can bend it with a; now k takes it up and down in a lift.

🎯 In this lesson

Add the + k number to get the full y = ax² + k. You’ll see it just slides the whole parabola straight up or down, parking the vertex at (0, k).

What k does

Whatever ax² works out to, k adds on at the end — so every point rises by k (or drops, if it’s negative). The shape doesn’t change at all; the whole U just elevator-rides. 🛗

  • k > 0 → slides up. k < 0 → slides down.
  • The vertex (the tip of the U) moves from (0, 0) to (0, k) — so you can read k straight off the graph: it’s the height of the tip.

🕹️ The full parabola machine

Both controls now: a bends, k lifts. The teal dot marks the vertex.

Missions:
  1. Lift the vertex to (0, 4) without touching the pink slider.
  2. Make a frown whose peak is at (0, 3).
  3. Drop the vertex below the x-axis. What sign is k?
  4. Make a skinny smile sitting with its tip at (0, −2).
💚 Easy win: a bends the U; k lifts it, with the vertex always at (0, k).

Parabolas: y = ax² + k 🥣

The shape of a thrown ball, a satellite dish, a water fountain. Two numbers again — one bends it, one lifts it.

🎯 In this lesson

You’ll own y = ax² + k: how a makes the U fatter, skinnier, or flipped upside-down, and how k slides the whole thing up and down. No factorising, no roots — just the shape.

Meet y = x²

The rule is “square your x.” Squaring always gives a positive answer (a negative times a negative is positive), so the curve sits above the x-axis and makes a symmetrical U called a parabola.

x−3−2−10123
y = x²9410149

Notice the mirror symmetry: −3 and +3 both give 9. The bottom of the U — here the point (0, 0) — is called the vertex (the turning point).

What the two numbers do

  • a — the stretch. Big a (like 3) → tall & skinny. Small a (like ½) → short & fat. And if a is negative, the U flips upside-down into a ∩ (a frown 🙁).
  • k — the lift. It just slides the whole parabola straight up (if k > 0) or down (if k < 0). The vertex moves to (0, k).

🕹️ The parabola machine

Play. Fatten it, flip it, lift it.

Missions:
  1. Make the skinniest U you can, then the fattest. (Which slider? Which direction?)
  2. Flip it into a frown ∩. What did you do to a?
  3. Lift the vertex to (0, 4) without touching the pink slider.
  4. Make a frown whose peak is at (0, 3).
💚 Easy win: a bends (size = skinny/fat, sign = smile/frown); k lifts (the vertex sits at (0, k)).

🚀 Bonus (advanced — just a peek)

There’s a third move: y = (x + h)² slides the parabola left and right. You don’t need it for Year 10, but it’s fun to see. Open Desmos, type y = (x + h)^2, add a slider for h, and drag — watch the whole U walk sideways. (Sneaky bit: + h moves it left, not right!)

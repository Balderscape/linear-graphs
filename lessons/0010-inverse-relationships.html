Inverse Relationships: y = a/x 🔀

Straight lines are done. Now the curves — starting with the trade-off curve, where the bigger one thing gets, the smaller the other has to be.

🎯 In this lesson

You’ll meet y = a/x — a curve in two pieces that gets closer and closer to the axes but never touches them. By the end you’ll know why it splits, what a does, and how to read it.

The see-saw rule

Some things trade off against each other. Share a pizza between more people → everyone gets a smaller slice. Drive a fixed distance faster → it takes less time. When one goes up, the other goes down — that’s an inverse relationship.

y = a ÷ x

“Pick an x, divide a by it — that’s your y.” The simplest one is y = 1/x. Watch what the numbers do:

See it? As x gets big, y gets tiny. As x shrinks toward 0, y explodes.

Two things that make it weird 👻

  • It’s in two separate pieces. Positive x gives positive y (top-right), negative x gives negative y (bottom-left). The curve can’t cross between them.
  • It never touches the axes. You can’t divide by 0, so there’s no point at x = 0. And y can get microscopically small but never actually hits 0. Lines a curve sneaks up on but never reaches are called asymptotes — here, the two axes.

🕹️ The trade-off machine

Drag a. Watch the curve swell out, and flip sides when a goes negative.

Missions:
  1. Make a bigger. Does the curve move closer to the origin or further away?
  2. Make a negative. Which two corners does the curve jump to?
  3. Can you ever get the curve to touch or cross an axis? (Nope — feel why.)
💚 Easy win: positive a → top-right & bottom-left. Negative a → top-left & bottom-right. Bigger a → curve sits further out.

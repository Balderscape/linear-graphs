# Linear Graphs Resources

All links verified live on 2026-07-03.

## Knowledge

- [Khan Academy: Forms of Linear Equations (Algebra 1 unit)](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/algebra/x2f8bb11595b61c86:forms-of-linear-equations)
  Full unit of videos + auto-marked practice: slope-intercept intro, graphing from the equation, writing equations from graphs/tables, converting forms. **Uses US terms** — their "slope" = gradient, their *b* = our *c*. Use for: any topic she wants explained a second way, plus free auto-marked practice.
- [Corbettmaths: y = mx + c (Video 191)](https://corbettmaths.com/2013/05/29/ymxc/)
  Short UK-notation video on what m and c mean. Use for: lesson 1 backup explainer.
- [Corbettmaths: Gradient of a Line](https://corbettmaths.com/2013/05/15/gradient-of-a-line/)
  Rise-over-run from a drawn line. Use for: lesson 2 backup explainer.
- [Corbettmaths: Drawing Graphs using Gradient and Intercept](https://corbettmaths.com/2013/04/20/drawing-graphs-using-gradient-and-intercept/)
  Plot straight from y = mx + c (start at c, step by m). Use for: lesson 3.
- [Corbettmaths: Equation of a Line — textbook exercise PDF](https://corbettmaths.com/2019/09/26/equation-of-a-line-textbook-exercise/)
  Free printable written practice. Use for: pen-and-paper drill after lessons 4-6.
- [Corbettmaths: y = graphs (Video 193, horizontal lines)](https://corbettmaths.com/2013/05/29/y-equals-graphs/)
  Horizontal lines y = k; page links onward to the x = graphs video (vertical). Use for: lesson 7.
- [Corbettmaths: Solving Simultaneous Equations Graphically](https://corbettmaths.com/2019/03/27/solving-simultaneous-equations-graphically/)
  Reading the solution from where two lines cross. Use for: lesson 8.
- [Maths Genie: Equation of a Line (GCSE grade 5)](https://mathsgenie.co.uk/gcse/maths/edexcel/grade-5-5-15-equation-of-a-line)
  Video + exam-style worksheets **with solutions**. Sibling pages (same URL scheme): `grade-5-5-14-gradient-of-a-line`, `grade-3-3-15-drawing-linear-graphs`, `grade-5-5-12-solving-simultaneous-equations-graphically`. NB: site moved to this URL scheme in 2025/26 — old `.html` links just redirect to the homepage. Use for: exam-style practice, esp. after lesson 9.
- [Desmos Graphing Calculator](https://www.desmos.com/calculator)
  Type `y = mx + c` and it offers sliders; plot two lines to see intersections. No account needed. Use for: checking any answer, free play.
- [Marbleslides: Lines (Desmos Classroom / Amplify)](https://teacher.desmos.com/marbleslides-lines)
  The classic game: edit line equations so marbles roll through stars — the best gradient/intercept intuition builder anywhere. **Needs a parent to create a free teacher account** and share a class code (plays via student.amplify.com). Use for: reward-play after lessons 1-3.
- [Dr Frost — free student accounts](https://www.drfrost.org/students)
  Free sign-up; question generators, video worked examples, real past-paper questions with instant feedback. Search skills like "Understanding y = mx + c", "Gradient of a Line". Topic pages sit behind the free login. Use for: extra drilling once the course is done.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [Free Math Help Forum](https://www.freemathhelp.com/forum/)
  Long-running, moderated homework-help forum with beginner-algebra sections; friendlier pacing than Stack Exchange for a 15-year-old. Open public forum: register with a parent, keep personal details out of posts.
- [Mathematics Stack Exchange](https://math.stackexchange.com/)
  High-quality but terse, adult-majority Q&A (minimum age 13). Use for: well-formed specific questions with working shown; parental oversight recommended.
- Best community of all here: **Dad + the kitchen table.** Amelia teaching Paul a lesson back ("explain why m = rise/run") is the highest-value practice available at zero cost.
